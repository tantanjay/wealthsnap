🛠️ How to "Visualise" the Financial Health Screen (Without Charts)
Since that screen is focused on numbers and advice, use these three high-impact visual techniques:

1. Dynamic Progress Bars (Linear Visuals)
Instead of just saying "10.8 months," place that number on a simple horizontal "Security Scale".

Visual: A thin bar that fills up from 0 to 12+ months.

Why: It gives the "number" a physical location. A user can see they are halfway to a 1-year safety net without needing to do the mental math.

2. "Impact" Badges and Icons
Instead of plain text for "Debt Drag," use a "Weight" or "Leak" icon.

Visual: A small red "minus" badge next to Debt Drag and a green "rocket" badge next to Investment Boost.

Why: This uses Color Psychology to tell the user if a number is "stealing" from them or "gifting" them time before they even read the label.

3. Visual "Advice" Blocks
Since this screen is heavy on advice, use Callout Boxes instead of standard paragraphs.

Visual: Put your most critical advice (e.g., "If you invest ₱35k/mo, Freedom arrives 6.4 years earlier") inside a distinct, lightly colored box with a "💡" or "🚀" icon.

Why: In a screen full of numbers, a "boxed" piece of advice acts as a visual anchor that the eye naturally jumps to first.

1. Interactive Micro-Insights
Tap-to-Expand Daily Mini-Dashboard: Instead of showing all details below, tapping a specific calendar date could reveal a small, overlaid summary of that day's top expense category or specific investment signal details (e.g., "Sold: 50 Shares of AREIT").

"Safe-to-Spend" Milestone: Add a subtle visual marker (like a small star or dot) on dates when the user reaches a specific financial goal or completes a "No-Spend" day to provide positive reinforcement.

2. Enhanced Trend Visualizations
Density Shading for Events: Use the "depth of color" not just for spending amounts but for the frequency of transactions. Darker shades could represent days with high transaction counts, helping users identify "busy" financial days.

Weekly Mini-Summaries: Add a narrow vertical column at the end of each week row showing a "Weekly Net" or "Weekly Burn Rate" to help users manage their pace mid-month.

3. Smart Filtering & Discovery
Search & Filter Toggles: Include small, unobtrusive icons for searching by keyword or filtering transactions by type (e.g., only "Needs" vs. "Wants") directly from the calendar view to increase trust and usability.

"Safe-to-Spend" Progress Bar: Turn the current static "Safe to Spend" number into a small progress bar that visually drains as the month progresses, making the remaining budget more tangible.

4. Utility & Control Features
Natural Language Quick-Add: Add a "plus" button that allows users to type simple phrases like "₱500 for gas tomorrow" to automatically populate future ghost forecasts.

Holiday/Event Awareness: Overlay public holidays (especially in the Philippines) or custom event markers (like "Pay Day" or "Stock Market Closed") to provide context for spending spikes.

5. Advanced Comparative Metrics
Last Year Variance: For power users, a small indicator could show if current spending on a specific date is significantly higher or lower than the same period last year.

Investment Signal Details: Use specific color-coded triangles for different types of investment moves, such as Gold for profit-taking sales and Red for stop-loss triggers.
